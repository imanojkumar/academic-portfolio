Smart Factory Decision System

Associated course: AI for Manufacturing Engineering
Format: Capstone brief
Suggested duration: 6 weeks

Abstract

Develop a practical proposal for an AI-supported manufacturing decision process using operational and sensor data.

Learning outcomes

On completion, participants should be able to:

  • Specify a decision system for a manufacturing environment from an ambiguous starting brief.
  • Select and justify the data, models and interfaces the system requires.
  • Address deployment realities: instrumentation gaps, operator trust and maintenance practice.
  • Present a proposal that an engineering manager could act on.

Brief

Teams specify a decision-support system for a manufacturing plant of their choosing, real or composite. The system must serve a decision that is currently made by judgement alone, and the team must establish that the decision is worth supporting before designing anything.

The emphasis is on specification quality rather than implementation. A working prototype is welcome but is not required and carries no additional credit on its own.

Deliverables

  • Decision definition -the decision supported, who makes it, how often, and the current basis
  • System specification -data sources, processing, model or rule logic, and interface
  • Deployment assessment -instrumentation gaps, operator trust, maintenance implications
  • Evidence plan -how the team would establish that the system improves the decision
  • Proposal presentation to an engineering management audience

Milestone schedule

Week Milestone
1 Decision selected and justified
2 Current-state assessment complete
3 Draft specification submitted for formative feedback
4 Deployment and evidence sections drafted
5 Full draft submitted
6 Final proposal and presentation

Assessment

Assessment criteria and weightings are set at course level and are recorded in the course record rather than duplicated here.
