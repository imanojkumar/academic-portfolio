case study

The Retail Growth Dashboard

Associated course: Business Analytics
Format: Student case and discussion questions
Suggested duration: 75 minutes

Abstract

A management team must decide which performance signals deserve action when revenue and customer metrics point in different directions.

Learning outcomes

By the end of this case discussion, participants should be able to:

  • Select performance measures appropriate to a stated decision rather than to data availability.
  • Recognise how measure selection shapes managerial attention and behaviour.
  • Identify measures that are widely reported but weakly connected to the decision at hand.
  • Design a concise reporting view and justify each inclusion and exclusion.

Case context

A retail management team has commissioned a growth dashboard. The analytics function has returned a view with thirty-one measures across five sections, assembled largely from what the existing systems can produce reliably. The team finds it comprehensive and unusable.

Underlying the request are three decisions the team actually makes each month: where to place incremental marketing spend, which underperforming stores to intervene in, and which product categories to expand or contract. None of the three is clearly served by the current view.

The decision

Participants redesign the dashboard around the three decisions. They must specify what to include, what to remove, and how each retained measure connects to an action. They should expect to defend the removals more than the inclusions.

Discussion questions

  1. For each of the three decisions, what would actually change the action taken?
  2. Which of the thirty-one measures would you remove, and what objection would you expect?
  3. Where does data availability appear to have driven measure selection?
  4. What behaviour would each of your retained measures encourage if managers were held to it?
  5. How would you present a measure whose movement is real but not actionable?

Suggested teaching sequence

Timings assume a 75-minute session and should be adjusted to the cohort.

Stage Minutes Focus
Framing 8 Establish the decision and the constraints on it
Small groups 22 Groups work the exhibits and form a position
Plenary 26 Positions are stated and tested against the evidence
Complication 11 Introduce the withheld detail and re-test the positions
Close 8 Generalise to the participants' own contexts
