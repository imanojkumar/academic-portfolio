From Data to Decisions

Associated course: Data-Driven Decision Making
Format: Presentation outline
Suggested duration: 60 minutes

Abstract

A lecture deck on structuring decisions, evaluating evidence and communicating recommendations.

Learning outcomes

By the end of this session, participants should be able to:

  • Trace the path from a business question to an analytical question and back to a decision.
  • Identify where analytical work commonly detaches from the decision it was meant to serve.
  • Frame an analytical result in terms of the action it supports.

Session outline

Segment Minutes Focus
Opening — a decision that was not made 8 Open with a situation in which good analysis produced no decision. Establish the session's question: what has to be true for analysis to change an action?
Framing the business question 12 Move from vague requests to answerable questions. Contrast a request for information with a request for a decision.
What the data can and cannot settle 12 Distinguish questions the available data can answer, questions it can answer weakly, and questions it cannot answer at all.
From result to recommendation 15 Work through the translation step: result, interpretation, implication, recommended action, and the conditions under which the recommendation changes.
Communicating under disagreement 8 How to present a result to an audience that has a prior commitment to a different conclusion.
Close and discussion 5 Return to the opening situation and invite the group to reconstruct the decision.

Delivery notes

The outline is written for a single continuous session. Where the session is split, the natural break falls after the third segment.

Segment timings are indicative. The discussion segments are the ones worth protecting if the session runs short.

Supporting materials

Slides and speaker notes are maintained separately and are not published in full.
