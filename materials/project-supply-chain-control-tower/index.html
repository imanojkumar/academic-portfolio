Supply-Chain Control Tower Project

Associated course: Agentic AI in Supply Chain
Format: Capstone brief
Suggested duration: 6 weeks

Abstract

Design an agent-enabled control tower that identifies disruptions, recommends actions and escalates exceptions.

Learning outcomes

On completion, participants should be able to:

  • Design a control-tower capability around specific operational decisions rather than around visibility.
  • Define the scope of automated action and the points of human authority.
  • Anticipate the organisational response to centralised visibility.
  • Present a staged implementation with defined evidence gates.

Brief

Teams design a supply-chain control tower for an organisation of their choosing. The common failure of such initiatives is to deliver visibility that changes no decision, so teams must begin from the decisions the capability is intended to improve and work backwards to the data required.

Where the design includes autonomous action, teams must state the scope of that authority explicitly and justify it.

Deliverables

  • Decision inventory - the operational decisions the control tower is intended to improve
  • Capability design - data, integration, alerting and action scope
  • Authority model - what acts automatically, what escalates, and on what basis
  • Organisational assessment - how affected teams are likely to respond to centralised visibility
  • Staged implementation plan with evidence gates

Milestone schedule

Week Milestone
1 Organisation and decision inventory agreed
2 Data and integration assessment complete
3 Draft capability design submitted for formative feedback
4 Authority model and organisational assessment drafted
5 Full draft submitted
6 Final design and presentation

Assessment

Assessment criteria and weightings are set at course level and are recorded in the course record rather than duplicated here.
