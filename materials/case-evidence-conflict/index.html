When the Evidence Conflicts

Associated course: Business Science
Format: Decision case
Suggested duration: 75 minutes

Abstract

A leadership team must reconcile experimental results, customer feedback and commercial pressure before approving a new initiative.

Learning outcomes

By the end of this case discussion, participants should be able to:

  • Reconcile evidence from sources of differing design, sample and reliability.
  • Identify where a conflict between evidence types reflects measurement rather than disagreement.
  • Recognise the influence of commercial pressure on the interpretation of ambiguous results.
  • Reach and defend a decision without waiting for evidence that will not arrive in time.

Case context

A product leadership team holds three bodies of evidence about a proposed change. A controlled experiment shows a small but statistically clear negative effect on a primary metric. Qualitative customer research is strongly positive, with participants describing the change as an obvious improvement. Commercial forecasting, built on the assumption that the change ships, has already been incorporated into the quarterly plan.

The experiment ran for two weeks on a segment that is not fully representative. The customer research involved fourteen participants recruited through an existing user panel. Neither weakness was raised when the evidence was first presented.

The decision

Participants advise the team on whether to ship, hold, or modify the change, and on what they would measure next. The decision must be made before the quarter closes; a further experiment of adequate power would take six weeks.

Discussion questions

  1. Do the three sources actually disagree, or are they measuring different things?
  2. Which weaknesses in each source are disqualifying, and which are merely limitations to state?
  3. How should the team treat a forecast that already assumes the outcome of the decision?
  4. What decision would you take if the commercial pressure were absent, and does that tell you anything?
  5. What is the smallest additional piece of evidence that would most change your view?

Suggested teaching sequence

Timings assume a 75-minute session and should be adjusted to the cohort.

Stage Minutes Focus
Framing 8 Establish the decision and the constraints on it
Small groups 22 Groups work the exhibits and form a position
Plenary 26 Positions are stated and tested against the evidence
Complication 11 Introduce the withheld detail and re-test the positions
Close 8 Generalise to the participants' own contexts
