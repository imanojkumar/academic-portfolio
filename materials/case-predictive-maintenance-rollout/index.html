The Predictive Maintenance Rollout

Associated course: AI for Manufacturing Engineering
Format: Engineering management case
Suggested duration: 90 minutes

Abstract

A manufacturing plant evaluates whether to expand an AI maintenance pilot despite uneven model performance across production lines.

Learning outcomes

By the end of this case discussion, participants should be able to:

  • Assess whether pilot performance justifies expansion across heterogeneous assets.
  • Identify the organisational and maintenance-practice conditions on which model value depends.
  • Evaluate uneven model performance across production lines as an engineering and a management problem.
  • Structure a staged rollout with defined evidence gates.

Case context

A manufacturing plant has piloted a predictive maintenance capability on two production lines. On the first line the models perform well and two significant failures were anticipated and avoided. On the second line performance is materially weaker: alerts are frequent, precision is poor, and maintenance staff have begun to disregard them.

The two lines differ in age, instrumentation density and maintenance history. The plant has six further lines, spanning a wider range of conditions than either pilot line. Central engineering has proposed full rollout on the strength of the first line's results.

The decision

Participants advise the plant engineering manager on whether to expand, and if so in what sequence and under what conditions. They must also decide what to do about the second line, where operator trust has already been damaged.

Exhibits

  • Exhibit A - Pilot performance by line, including alert precision and recall
  • Exhibit B - Asset register with age, instrumentation and failure history

  • Exhibit D - Central engineering rollout proposal

Discussion questions

  1. What explains the performance gap between the two lines, and which explanations can be tested?
  2. Is the second line a model problem, a data problem, or a maintenance-practice problem?
  3. What evidence gate would you set before each stage of expansion?
  4. How is operator trust recovered once alerts have been disregarded?
  5. What would you say to central engineering about their proposal?

Suggested teaching sequence

Timings assume a 90-minute session and should be adjusted to the cohort.

Stage Minutes Focus
Framing 9 Establish the decision and the constraints on it
Small groups 27 Groups work the exhibits and form a position
Plenary 31 Positions are stated and tested against the evidence
Complication 14 Introduce the withheld detail and re-test the positions
Close 9 Generalise to the participants' own contexts
