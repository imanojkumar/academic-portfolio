# Designing a Digital Transformation Roadmap

**Associated course:** Digital Transformation for Managers  
**Format:** Presentation outline  
**Suggested duration:** 75 minutes

## Abstract

A manager-focused presentation on priorities, sequencing, operating-model change and value delivery.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this session, participants should be able to:

- Distinguish a transformation roadmap from a technology procurement schedule.
- Sequence initiatives according to dependency and organisational capacity.
- Identify the conditions that cause roadmaps to lose credibility internally.

## Session outline

| Segment | Minutes | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Opening — why roadmaps fail | 8 | Establish the common failure modes: sequencing by enthusiasm, capacity assumed rather than measured, and benefits committed before dependencies are understood. |
| Establishing the current position | 12 | Assessing existing capability honestly, including the parts of the organisation that will not move at the proposed pace. |
| Sequencing by dependency | 15 | Building the sequence from what must precede what, rather than from what is most visible. |
| Capacity and absorption | 12 | The limit is rarely funding. Treat organisational absorption as the binding constraint. |
| Governance and revision | 15 | How the roadmap is reviewed, and how it is changed without losing credibility. |
| Close and discussion | 8 | Participants critique a sequenced roadmap and identify its weakest dependency. |

## Delivery notes

The outline is written for a single continuous session. Where the session is split, the natural break falls after the third segment.

Segment timings are indicative. The discussion segments are the ones worth protecting if the session runs short.
