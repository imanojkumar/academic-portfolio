# Operations Modelling Workbook Guide

**Associated course:** Operations Analytics  
**Format:** Workbook guide  
**Suggested duration:** Self-paced

## Abstract

A practical guide for structuring capacity, inventory and scenario models in a spreadsheet.

## Purpose

A guide to the operations modelling workbook: what it contains, how the model is structured, and how to use it in teaching without the mechanics of the spreadsheet displacing the operational reasoning.

## What this resource contains

- Workbook structure and sheet-by-sheet purpose
- Model assumptions and where they are recorded
- Worked example with a full parameter set
- Sensitivity exercises and the behaviour each is intended to reveal
- Guidance on extending the model without breaking its assumption register

## How to use it

Introduce the workbook only after participants have reasoned about the operation without it. The common failure is that the spreadsheet becomes the subject of the session.

The sensitivity exercises are the most valuable component and are frequently omitted for time. If the session must be shortened, reduce the worked example rather than the sensitivity work.

## Adaptation

The resource is intended to be adapted. Where it is adapted for a specific institution or cohort, the adaptation should preserve the sequence of questions rather than the particular wording.
