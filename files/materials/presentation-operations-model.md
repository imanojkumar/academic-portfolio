# Building an Operations Analytics Model

**Associated course:** Operations Analytics  
**Format:** Presentation outline  
**Suggested duration:** 75 minutes

## Abstract

A guided presentation covering process measures, forecasts, capacity and scenario analysis.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this session, participants should be able to:

- Structure an operations model around the decisions it is intended to support.
- Select a level of detail proportionate to the decision and the data available.
- Test a model's behaviour at the boundaries of its assumptions.

## Session outline

| Segment | Minutes | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Opening — the purpose of the model | 8 | A model is built for a decision. Establish what changes if the model says one thing rather than another. |
| Choosing the level of detail | 15 | Where additional detail improves the decision, and where it only improves the appearance of rigour. |
| Building the structure | 18 | Flows, constraints, variability and the points at which the model is most sensitive. |
| Testing at the boundaries | 15 | Behaviour under extreme inputs, and what unrealistic model behaviour reveals about the assumptions. |
| Handing the model over | 12 | Documentation, assumptions register, and the conditions under which the model should no longer be used. |
| Close and discussion | 7 | Participants identify the assumption in their own context most likely to break first. |

## Delivery notes

The outline is written for a single continuous session. Where the session is split, the natural break falls after the third segment.

Segment timings are indicative. The discussion segments are the ones worth protecting if the session runs short.
