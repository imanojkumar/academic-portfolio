# Autonomous Replenishment Under Pressure

**Associated course:** Agentic AI in Supply Chain  
**Format:** Operational decision case  
**Suggested duration:** 90 minutes

## Abstract

A supply-chain leader must decide how much authority to give an AI agent during a period of volatile demand.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this case discussion, participants should be able to:

- Reason about the appropriate scope of authority for an autonomous agent in an operational process.
- Identify the control points at which human judgement should be retained.
- Evaluate the trade-off between responsiveness and reversibility in agent-driven decisions.
- Design an escalation and override mechanism proportionate to the operational risk.

## Case context

A distribution business has deployed an agent-based replenishment system across a regional network. In stable conditions the system has performed well: service levels have held and working capital has fallen. The agent has authority to place replenishment orders within a value threshold and to reallocate stock between sites without approval.

Demand has then become volatile. The agent has responded by increasing order frequency and by reallocating stock more aggressively between sites. Two regional managers have complained that stock is being moved away from them faster than they can react. A third site has received an order that, while individually within threshold, formed part of a sequence that materially exceeded the intended commitment.

## The decision

Participants advise the supply-chain leader, who must decide within the week whether to widen the agent's authority, narrow it, or restructure it. Simply switching the system off is available but carries its own cost, and the leader has to explain the decision to both the regional managers and the executive sponsor who approved the deployment.

## Discussion questions

1. Was the threshold breached? Distinguish between the letter of the authority model and its intent.
2. Which of the agent's actions were errors, and which were correct actions that produced uncomfortable outcomes?
3. Where would you place a human checkpoint, and what would it cost in responsiveness?
4. How should authority be expressed so that a sequence of individually permitted actions cannot aggregate into an impermissible one?
5. What should the leader say to the regional managers, and what should change in how they were prepared for this deployment?

## Suggested teaching sequence

Timings assume a 90-minute session and should be adjusted to the cohort.

| Stage | Minutes | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Framing | 9 | Establish the decision and the constraints on it |
| Small groups | 27 | Groups work the exhibits and form a position |
| Plenary | 31 | Positions are stated and tested against the evidence |
| Complication | 14 | Introduce the withheld detail and re-test the positions |
| Close | 9 | Generalise to the participants' own contexts |
