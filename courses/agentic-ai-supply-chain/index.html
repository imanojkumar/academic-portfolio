Agentic AI in Supply Chain

Agentic AI in Supply Chain

A specialised course on designing, evaluating and governing AI agents across planning, procurement, logistics, inventory and supply-chain control processes.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
10 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Online / Hybrid
Credits
3
Intended audience
Supply-chain, operations and engineering management students and professionals exploring intelligent workflow automation.
Prerequisites
Introductory knowledge of supply-chain processes; prior AI experience is not required.

Learning outcomes

  • Map supply-chain decisions suitable for AI-agent support.
  • Design agent roles, tools, data access and human controls.
  • Evaluate reliability, coordination and operational risk.
  • Develop performance measures for agent-enabled workflows.
  • Propose a phased implementation for a supply-chain use case.

Module structure

  1. Supply-chain decisions and agent opportunities — Planning, procurement, inventory, logistics and service.
  2. Agent design and workflow orchestration — Roles, tools, memory, hand-offs and escalation.
  3. Data and enterprise-system integration — Operational data, APIs and process constraints.
  4. Reliability, controls and governance — Human oversight, exception handling and accountability.
  5. Pilot design and scale-up — Metrics, operating change and implementation roadmap.

Assessment methods

  • Process opportunity map — 25%
  • Agent workflow design — 35%
  • Supply-chain pilot proposal — 40%
