Business Science

Business Science

An interdisciplinary course combining business understanding, analytical methods, experimentation and systems thinking to address complex organisational problems.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
12 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Hybrid
Credits
3
Intended audience
Students and professionals seeking an integrated approach to business problems that cross functions, data and organisational systems.
Prerequisites
Introductory business statistics or equivalent experience is helpful.

Learning outcomes

  • Diagnose complex business problems using structured inquiry.
  • Combine quantitative and qualitative evidence.
  • Design experiments and evaluate managerial interventions.
  • Model interactions across business functions and systems.
  • Translate findings into feasible organisational action.

Module structure

  1. Business science as an integrated discipline — Problem contexts, evidence and model-based reasoning.
  2. Causal questions and experimentation — Experiments, natural experiments and practical limitations.
  3. Systems and stakeholder analysis — Interdependencies, feedback and organisational behaviour.
  4. Decision models under uncertainty — Scenarios, trade-offs and robust choices.
  5. From evidence to implementation — Recommendations, adoption and evaluation.

Assessment methods

  • Problem diagnosis brief — 25%
  • Experiment design assignment — 30%
  • Integrated business science project — 45%
