AI for Business

AI for Business

A managerial introduction to artificial intelligence focused on use-case selection, business value, operating implications, risk and implementation choices.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
10 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Online / Hybrid / Executive Workshop
Credits
3
Intended audience
Managers, analysts, consultants and business students who need to evaluate and lead AI-enabled initiatives.
Prerequisites
No programming prerequisite. Familiarity with business processes is expected.

Learning outcomes

  • Explain major AI capabilities in business terms.
  • Identify and prioritise viable business use cases.
  • Evaluate data, technology and organisational readiness.
  • Assess value, risk and implementation alternatives.
  • Develop a practical AI adoption roadmap.

Module structure

  1. Understanding AI in business — Capabilities, limitations and the evolving business landscape.
  2. Use-case discovery and prioritisation — Value pools, feasibility and portfolio choices.
  3. Data, platforms and operating models — Foundations for implementation and scale.
  4. Generative and autonomous AI applications — Business workflows, human oversight and performance.
  5. Implementation roadmap — Governance, change, measurement and continuous improvement.

Assessment methods

  • AI opportunity scan — 25%
  • Use-case business case — 35%
  • AI implementation roadmap — 40%
