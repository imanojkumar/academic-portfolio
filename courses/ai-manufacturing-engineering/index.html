AI for Manufacturing Engineering

AI for Manufacturing Engineering

An applied course linking artificial intelligence with manufacturing engineering decisions, process performance, quality, maintenance and production systems.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
14 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Hybrid
Credits
4
Intended audience
Manufacturing, industrial, mechanical and engineering management students and professionals.
Prerequisites
Basic manufacturing systems knowledge and introductory statistics.

Learning outcomes

  • Identify manufacturing decisions that can benefit from AI.
  • Prepare sensor and production data for analysis.
  • Evaluate quality, maintenance and process applications.
  • Connect AI models with manufacturing workflows and controls.
  • Design an implementation proposal for a smart manufacturing use case.

Module structure

  1. AI opportunities in manufacturing — Production, quality, maintenance, energy and flow.
  2. Industrial data foundations — Sensors, historians, traceability and data quality.
  3. Predictive quality and maintenance — Failure patterns, anomaly detection and decision thresholds.
  4. Process optimisation and intelligent control — Operational recommendations and closed-loop considerations.
  5. Smart manufacturing implementation — Architecture, workforce, risk and value realisation.

Assessment methods

  • Manufacturing data exercise — 25%
  • Applied AI case analysis — 30%
  • Smart manufacturing project — 45%
