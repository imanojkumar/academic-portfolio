Business Analytics

Business Analytics

A practice-oriented course that develops the ability to frame business questions, analyse data, interpret models and communicate evidence for managerial action.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
12 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Online / Hybrid
Credits
3
Intended audience
Business management, engineering management and early-career professionals who need practical analytical decision skills.
Prerequisites
Basic spreadsheet skills; no advanced programming required.

Learning outcomes

  • Frame business problems as answerable analytical questions.
  • Prepare and examine structured business data.
  • Select suitable descriptive, predictive and prescriptive approaches.
  • Interpret analytical results for managerial decisions.
  • Communicate recommendations through clear visual and written narratives.

Module structure

  1. Business questions and analytical thinking — Problem framing, decision context and measures of success.
  2. Data preparation and exploration — Data quality, summaries, patterns and visual analysis.
  3. Predictive analysis for management — Forecasting, classification and practical model interpretation.
  4. Decision models and scenarios — What-if analysis, optimisation and uncertainty.
  5. Communicating analytical recommendations — Dashboards, executive narratives and implementation choices.

Assessment methods

  • Applied analysis exercises — 30%
  • Business case report — 30%
  • Final analytics project — 40%
