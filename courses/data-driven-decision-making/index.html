Data-Driven Decision Making

Data-Driven Decision Making

A managerial course on using data, evidence and structured judgement to improve decisions while recognising uncertainty, bias and organisational constraints.

Programme level
Graduate / Postgraduate
Duration
8 weeks
Delivery format
Classroom / Online / Hybrid / Executive Workshop
Credits
2
Intended audience
Graduate and postgraduate students, managers and technical leaders responsible for evidence-based choices.
Prerequisites
No specialist technical prerequisite.

Learning outcomes

  • Distinguish information, evidence and managerial judgement.
  • Define decision criteria and relevant measures.
  • Interpret uncertainty and common analytical limitations.
  • Challenge assumptions and reduce decision bias.
  • Present an evidence-based recommendation to stakeholders.

Module structure

  1. Decision quality and evidence — Decision frames, criteria and information value.
  2. Interpreting data and uncertainty — Variation, confidence and practical significance.
  3. Bias, incentives and organisational context — Judgement traps and stakeholder dynamics.
  4. Scenario and trade-off analysis — Alternatives, sensitivity and robust choices.
  5. Communicating decisions — Recommendation structure, visual evidence and action.

Assessment methods

  • Decision memo — 30%
  • Evidence critique — 30%
  • Final decision presentation — 40%
